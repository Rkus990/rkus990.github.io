Research Statement

Automated Functional Synthesis is the problem of synthesizing outputs as functions of inputs, while satisfying a declarative relational specification between inputs and outputs. In other words, provided synthesis of such a system is complete, it is guaranteed to meet the high-level specifications it is designed for, and hence correct-by-construction.

Recent advances in this problem has served as a meeting place of techniques rich in knowledge compilation, automated reasoning and learning.

While the black-box AI systems have gained upsurge, it is important to realize that they are far from being correct and increasingly even more so in cases when they run into safety critical pipelines.

Complexity-theoretic arguments indicate that it is extremely unlikely that the problem of synthesis has any polynomial-time algorithm. Our work focuses on constructing systems that can achieve correctness-by-construction. What makes these algorithms tick? What are the open problems? See this!
